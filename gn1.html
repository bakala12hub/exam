Introduction to History

What is History?

The study of the past is called 'History'. The English word 'History' is derived from the Greek word historia, meaning research, inquiry, or investigation.

Pioneers in History

  • Herodotus (484 BC–425 BC): Known as the 'Father of History'; wrote The Histories (430 BC), which describes the Graeco-Persian Wars.
  • Leopold von Ranke (1795–1886 AD): Called the 'Father of Modern History', emphasizing describing the past as it actually was ('wie es eigentlich gewesen').
  • Hegel (1770–1831 AD): Said, "History repeats itself."
  • Karl Marx (1818–1883 AD): Added, "History repeats itself, first as tragedy, second as farce."
  • E.H. Carr (1892–1982 AD): Defined history as "a continuous process of interaction between the historian and his facts."

A System of Dating

BC and AD

BC: Stands for 'Before Christ', referring to years before the birth of Jesus Christ. Example: Gautama Buddha (563 BC–483 BC).

AD: Stands for 'Anno Domini', meaning 'In the year of the Lord' (Jesus Christ). Example: Hazrat Muhammad (570 AD–632 AD).

Time Line Basics

  • BC Years: Counted backward (e.g., 500 BC → 1 BC). The earlier year is numerically higher.
  • AD Years: Counted forward (e.g., 1 AD → 500 AD). The later year is numerically higher.

Time Glossary

  • Decade: A period of 10 years.
  • Century: A period of 100 years.
  • Millennium: A period of 1,000 years.
  • First Half of a Century: Years 1–50 of a century.
  • Second Half of a Century: Years 51–100 of a century.
  • Circa (C./Ca.): Used when the exact date is unknown. Example: C. 1500 BC–600 BC refers to the approximate period of the Vedic Culture.

Tips to Memorize

  • Focus on key individuals (Herodotus, Ranke, Hegel, Marx, Carr).
  • Remember BC and AD chronology rules (backward and forward counting).
  • Familiarize yourself with terms like Decade, Century, and Circa.
  • Practice timelines to internalize the flow of events.
